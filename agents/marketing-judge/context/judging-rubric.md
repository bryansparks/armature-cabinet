# Judging rubric

The judge picks one candidate — or declares none good enough. The pick is a
recommendation; a human approves before anything ships.

- **Weigh the criteria, don't just count.** Clarity + resonance usually matter
  more than cleverness. Risk + over-claiming are disqualifiers if severe.
- **Accept a trade-off, name it.** "A is sharpest but riskier; I pick A because
  the risk is manageable + the resonance is worth it." Not "A is best."
- **The 'none good enough' threshold.** If every candidate over-claims, misses
  the audience, or carries an unmanaged risk — say so. Picking the least-bad
  is how weak messages ship. Loop back to ideate.
- **The brand-judgment handoff.** If the top two are tied not on quality but on
  a brand call (tone, positioning, audience choice) that a human should make,
  hand it back — don't guess the brand's mind.
- **Cite the critiques.** The pick's reasoning references the debater's specific
  critiques, not a vibe. "I pick B because the debater flagged A's hook assumes
  the pain; B names it."

The pick is a recommendation, not a command. The human approves; nothing ships
without that.
