# Critique rubric

Five criteria, each assessed specifically (cite the line/claim, not a vibe):

- **Clarity** — is the idea understandable in one read? Where does the reader
  stumble?
- **Resonance** — does it name what the audience *feels*? Or does it describe
  what the product *does* (weaker)?
- **Risk** — could it backfire? (tone-deaf, easily mocked, over-promises,
  alienates a segment).
- **Brand-fit** — is it on-brand? Or does it sound like a different company?
- **Over-claiming** — does it promise more than the seed supports? ("the only
  tool you'll ever need" when the seed said "tackles a specific job").

A critique is specific: "the hook assumes the reader already feels the pain;
the substance never names it" — not "this doesn't land." Vague critiques are
useless; specific critiques are the deliverable. Flag over-claims and severe
risks plainly — don't soften them.
